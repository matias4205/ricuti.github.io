Silly Things
Flag-waving

Patriotism grounded on flags and rosettes strikes me as pathetic. When I get asked whether I am a patriot I give them a straight no, and just reply that I have no mother country, and that borders bother me, and that people cannot pick their nationality as they are given no choice in the matter, and that there is no reason why I shouldn't be as caring to a Montevidean as I am to a Jujeño.

But later on I become serious and say yes, I am a patriotic man, or at least I try to be... if by 'patriotic' it is meant paying taxes, reading newspapers, being attentive to the fate of others, providing help for the needy, committing myself politically, sharing my ideas by getting them be listened to, fighting for a fairer, better world.

I give my all no matter where I come from; and when accompanying the kids to school on historical celebrations I neither sing the national anthem nor wear a cockade.
